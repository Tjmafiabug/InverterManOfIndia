Why should an inverter just sit idle, waiting for power failure? Why can’t it work like a smart PCU (Power Conditioning Unit)&nbsp; one that runs appliances, charges batteries, and even integrates solar power seamlessly?</p><p>This question gave birth to the Brainy S 2000 / 24V, a product years ahead of its time.</p><h3><br></h3><figure id="figure-ze22lp872kmfqvymyz"><img id="ze22lp872kmfqvymyz" src="/InverterManOfIndia/assets/1aaEW229ZCsWWhsErpZoocdxPAb3x8jBqsXcDG0rtPuN0LrAAc9wILFIWibG0pZJ.png" alt="" title="" class="object-cover bg-gray-100 rounded-2xl md:rounded-3xl lg:rounded-4xl" style="aspect-ratio: 16/9; object-position: center center;" data-corners="default" data-aspect="16:9" data-caption="" data-positionx="undefined" data-positiony="undefined" data-fullwidth="false" data-mce-src="/InverterManOfIndia/assets/1aaEW229ZCsWWhsErpZoocdxPAb3x8jBqsXcDG0rtPuN0LrAAc9wILFIWibG0pZJ.png" data-mce-style="aspect-ratio: 16/9; object-position: center center;"></figure><h3><br>A New Category Was Born</h3><p>While most companies were struggling to make even basic sinewave inverters, Su-Kam under Kunwer’s leadership launched the Brainy S — a true Solar PCU.</p><p>It could run 15–16 computers at once, making it perfect for schools and offices.<br>It could power printers, refrigerators, microwaves, and more — loads that people never thought an inverter could handle reliably.<br>It used solar power along with grid supply to reduce electricity bills — a concept hardly anyone spoke of back then.<br>This was 2018, when the words green energy and solar hybrid were buzzwords on paper, not in real homes and offices. Yet, Kunwer Sachdev made it a reality.</p><p><br></p><figure id="figure-jm08212jgemfqvz784"><img id="jm08212jgemfqvz784" src="/InverterManOfIndia/assets/2dCUkGjN6AI2MDoywTpKadfDZLPN7mqHKzgQMpj4MTmvhqJeGqPTXZujsHyNXKov.png" alt="" title="" class="object-cover bg-gray-100 rounded-2xl md:rounded-3xl lg:rounded-4xl" style="aspect-ratio: 16/9; object-position: 0% 50%;" data-corners="default" data-aspect="16:9" data-caption="" data-positionx="0%" data-positiony="50%" data-fullwidth="false" data-mce-src="/InverterManOfIndia/assets/2dCUkGjN6AI2MDoywTpKadfDZLPN7mqHKzgQMpj4MTmvhqJeGqPTXZujsHyNXKov.png" data-mce-style="aspect-ratio: 16/9; object-position: 0% 50%;"></figure><p><br>The Thinking Behind It<br>Kunwer didn’t just make products; he solved problems.</p><p>He saw how small businesses lost productivity when computers went off during power cuts.<br>He noticed how homes wanted more than just lightbulbs running&nbsp; families wanted fridges, microwaves, and daily comforts to keep working.<br>He realized India needed affordable solar solutions that blended grid and renewable power without complexity.<br>Brainy S became the answer a PCU that thinks for you, automatically choosing the best power source, protecting appliances, and saving money.</p><p>&nbsp;<br>Laying the Foundation for the Future<br>The Brainy S 2000 was not just another inverter. It was a category creator.</p><h3>And soon, Su-Kam pushed boundaries further with advanced models like the COLOSSAL 5 KVA / 96V, bringing features like:</h3><p>Zero changeover time<br>Built-in galvanic isolation transformers<br>Static bypass<br>Smart connectivity with Wi-Fi, GSM, and Bluetooth<br>These weren’t just specifications. They were Kunwer Sachdev’s vision of a future where power backup would merge with intelligence, connectivity, and renewable energy.</p><figure id="figure-za8imu40xsmfqw04kl"><img id="za8imu40xsmfqw04kl" src="/InverterManOfIndia/assets/1bsnKKxrQFrLxTeEGJ5iRjXMcStW0NB6mHm25we0L08rCnybLiryEeQnfeUFXwWl.png" alt="" title="" class="object-cover bg-gray-100 rounded-2xl md:rounded-3xl lg:rounded-4xl" style="aspect-ratio: 16/9; object-position: center center;" data-corners="default" data-aspect="16:9" data-caption="" data-positionX="undefined" data-positionY="undefined" data-fullWidth="false"/></figure><p>&nbsp;<br>A Legacy of Firsts<br>Looking back, what makes the Brainy S story special is not just the technology — it’s the timing.</p><p>When others thought it can’t be done, Kunwer thought why not?.<br>When the world was waiting for solar to become mainstream, he created a product that brought solar into everyday homes.<br>When the industry followed trends, he set them.</p><p>Today, many companies boast of hybrid inverters and solar PCUs. But the truth is Kunwer Sachdev had already built it back in 2018.</p><p>&nbsp;<br>&nbsp;The Brainy S 2000 / 24V was not just an inverter; it was a statement.<br>A statement that India could innovate.
