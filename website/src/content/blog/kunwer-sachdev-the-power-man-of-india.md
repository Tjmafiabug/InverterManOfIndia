Power cuts weren’t just annoying; they were disruptive, constant, and deeply woven into everyday life in the 90s.</p><p>And you remember Kunwer Sachdev standing at the crossroads of that chaos, seeing something others didn't.</p><p>He wasn’t born into the power industry. He started as a TV dealer—young, driven, full of questions. He watched as people bought televisions they couldn't always use because the electricity simply wouldn't stay. That frustration sparked something in him. Something urgent. Something visionary.</p><p>That spark gave birth to Su-Kam in 1998. Back then, inverters were clunky, unreliable, and misunderstood. But Kunwer didn't just want to build another inverter—he wanted to reimagine the way power backup worked for Indian homes. He introduced technologies the market had never seen before—sine wave inverters, battery chargers, and later, even hybrid solar systems..</p><p>But then, as it sometimes happens in business, paths changed. Kunwer Sachdev eventually stepped away from Su-Kam—not with regret, but with purpose. Because for a mind like his, one mountain climbed only reveals another waiting.</p><p>Today, he leads Su-vastika, a company that reflects not just innovation—but evolution. This isn’t just Su-Kam 2.0. It’s smarter, sharper, and more sustainable.
